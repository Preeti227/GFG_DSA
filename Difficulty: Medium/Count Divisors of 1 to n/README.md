<h2><a href="https://www.geeksforgeeks.org/problems/james-bond-and-divisors5539/1">Count Divisors of 1 to n</a></h2><h3>Difficulty Level : Difficulty: Medium</h3><hr><div class="problems_problem_content__Xm_eO" style="--text-color: var(--problem-text-color);"><p><span style="font-size: 18px;">Given an integer <strong>n</strong>, find the sum of the count of <strong>divisors</strong> for each number from 1 to n.</span></p>
<p><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Examples :</strong></span></p>
<pre><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong>Input: </strong>n = 2
<strong>Output: </strong>3
<strong>Explanation:<br></strong>1 has 1 divisor: {1}.
2 has 2 divisors: {1, 2}. 
The total count is 1 + 2 = 3.</span>
</pre>
<pre><span style="font-size: 18px;"><strong style="font-size: 18px;">Input: </strong><span style="font-size: 18px;">n = 5
</span><strong style="font-size: 18px;">Output: </strong><span style="font-size: 14pt;">10</span>
<strong style="font-size: 18px;">Explanation:<br></strong><span style="font-size: 18px;">1 has 1 divisor: {1}.
2 has 2 divisors: {1, 2}. <br>3 has 2 divisors: {1, 3}.<br>4 has 3 divisors: {1, 2, 4}.<br>5 has 2 divisors: {1, 5}.
The total count is 1 + 2 + 2 + 3 + 2 = 10.</span></span>
</pre>
